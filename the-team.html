Jane Bennett

Being recommended in the Legal 500 as a "careful and thoughtful" Court of Protection specialist, Jane has over 17 years' experience working with Private Clients and over the last 10 years, has built a practice which supports vulnerable clients to identify and meet their legal needs.

She was a partner in London City/Westminster law firms for several years prior to setting up her own business in Sevenoaks in 2012.

Being very experienced in handling financial and care / welfare matters under the Court of Protection, Jane has a significant number of disabled and/or vulnerable clients for whom she acts as financial deputy and professional trustee. She also advises lay deputies and trustees.

Jane acts as an expert witness in catastrophic injury and high value claims in relation to professional deputy and trustee matters and regularly trains fellow professionals in this field.

Jane has a good eye for the details, and always insists on accuracy. She ensures the team maintains a client-centred and empathetic approach at all times.

Lorna

Lorna Halfpenny-Beeby

Lorna qualified as a Solicitor in England and Wales in 2004. She has worked in both the public law and private client sectors.

Prior to her admittance to the roll she obtained a BSc (Hons) in Psychology. Having spent several years working with the elderly she then proceeded to gain first hand experience of the issues faced by those with catastrophic brain injuries, spending 4 years working in a transitional living unit and then as part of a community rehabilitation team. Having joined JE Bennett Law, Lorna’s experience and knowledge leaves her well placed to assist vulnerable clients with the management and supervision of their property and affairs. Lorna has first hand experience of the effects of a spinal cord injury on a family member.

In her free time she has involvement with a registered charity R.E.A.D.Y (Recreation and Easy Access for Disabled Youth).

Katie qualified as a Solicitor in England and Wales in January 2012 and is a former criminal defence solicitor.  Katie is an experienced litigator in the criminal Courts and has aptitude and insight in working with vulnerable people in the Court arena.  Having learnt of the Court of Protection a few years into her career, Katie made the move to JE Bennett Law in order to specialise in this field as she recognised its crucial role to protect the most vulnerable members of society.  Katie has said that she became a solicitor with the objective of helping and empowering vulnerable people to have access to justice and she feels that she has now achieved that ambition in a role she describes as "truly humbling and hugely rewarding".
