Court of protection

Jane Bennett and the team advise the lay deputy appointed by the Court of Protection to manage the property and affairs or the health and welfare of a protected individual. Jane Bennett acts as professional deputy for property and affairs, for over 30 clients, and acts as professional panel deputy for the Court of Protection having been appointed to the national Court panel in June 2015. Jane also advises on deprivation of liberty and health and welfare matters, and frequently works with family members and/or the local authority to safeguard vulnerable people from financial abuse, neglect/physical or other forms of abuse, including investigating and advising regarding allegations against attorneys under Enduring/General/Lasting Powers of Attorney. Jane also acts as professional trustee for compensation protection trusts for the vulnerable and in particular, regarding trusts for spinally injured clients.

Elderly client law

Including advice regarding General/Enduring/Lasting Powers of Attorney; Wills, Probate and advice regarding care in the community and when you may be eligible to have it (link etc). As well as being able to advise you on your loved one’s care home contract and point out to you clauses that may cause you or your family challenges in future, we can advise you on local authority care funding matters and alert you to instances where you or your loved one may be entitled to NHS continuing care funding. The team has a growing profile locally and is committed to raising awareness of dementia and Alzheimer’s disease, often being involved in fundraising in this area.
